Inheritance via Taxonomy¶

Inheritance via Taxonomy is a feature that allows an article or product to receive predefined attributes set for a taxonomy.

Description:
  • Inheritance defines a taxonomy, arranging the concepts from generalized to specialized.
  • User can set the editable attributes and those that are inherited from the taxonomy.
Related topics:
  • Linking Objects
Application:

  • Example:

    Let’s take for instance the energy drink “Red Bull”. It falls under Food & Beverages > Beverages > Energy Drinks. It is common for Beverages to have the properties such as ingredients and important notice so it is necessary for Energy Drinks to inherit such properties.

How to do: Inheritance via Taxonomy¶

Inheriting a subset of attributes from a taxonomy is easy, and here’s how to do it.


  1. On the home page, click the Gear icon to go to Settings.


  1. Click the Taxonomy Configuration button.


  1. Click on the “+” icon to create a new taxonomy.


  1. Enter a name for the taxonomy then add property collection



  1. Select the type of coupling, enter the default value, and click Save.


  1. Go to PIM, hover on the “+” icon, and select your product.


  1. Click Add Taxonomy.



  1. Attributes under “Dynamic Coupling” are not editable as these values are inherited.


  1. Attributes under “Tight Coupling” can be updated.
