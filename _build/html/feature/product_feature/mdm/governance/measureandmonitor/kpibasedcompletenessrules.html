KPI-based Completeness Rules¶

The KPI-based Completeness Rules is a data governance feature that sets up rules to assess the completeness of an Article or a Product with respect to a KPI.

Key Term:
  • Key Performance Indicator (KPI): is a measurement used to determine the quality of product data added into the system in relation to a set standard.
Description:
  • KPIs demonstrate the accuracy of the products on the basis of an article’s attributes, taxonomy, and tags.
  • KPIs check the following data accuracy measures: Accuracy, Completeness, Conformity and Uniqueness.
  • KPIs can be drilled down on the basis of Taxonomy and Tags.
  • The KPI: Completeness, as the name suggests, ensures that the product data are complete in all aspects.
Related topics:
  • Tag Management
  • Attribute Management
  • Manage Taxonomy
  • Monitor Data Quality
Application:

  • Data Completeness is one of the most important data quality measures. It is one of the most important factors for KPIs.

  • Example:

    Supplier A with 100 fashion products sets up a rule stating that the Name Attribute can’t be left blank. This rule will be applicable to all of 100 fashion products. If there are violations, they would be reflected in the graph.

How to do: KPI-based Completeness Rules¶

To do this feature, follow these steps:


  1. Log in and go to Administration.


  1. Click on the Data Governance tab.


  1. Click on KPIs.


  1. Click on the “+” icon to create a new KPI.


  1. Fill out the name and click on the Create button.


  1. Once the KPI is created, the new KPI will be shown on the list. Click on the Edit button on the right.


  1. On the KPI Details page, select a Class and Taxonomy, which will use the KPI. Click the Apply button.



  1. For the Drill down levels, add Tags or Taxonomies, where the KPI is to be categorized into.


  1. Click on the Completeness tab and click on the + Add Rule button to create a new rule.


  1. Provide the details for the Label, Attributes, and Tags to which the rule will be applied.



  1. Click the Save button.


Note: A notification will confirm that the rule and the KPI have been created. Click OK to continue.
